Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "Recovering Relativity and Standard Model Structure from Observer-Overlap Consistency," for publication in Foundations of Physics.

This submission is a shorter and more concise successor to the previously submitted full-length OPH manuscript, "Observers Are All You Need: Observer-Patch Holography as a Fundamental Theory." The earlier version aimed to present the entire OPH program in one place. The present manuscript is intentionally narrower. It removes the broader philosophical and programmatic material and concentrates on the falsifiable core of the framework.

In particular, the compact paper focuses on the derivation chain from observer-overlap consistency to Lorentz kinematics, Einstein dynamics, compact gauge reconstruction, the exact Standard Model gauge structure with its global quotient, the hypercharge lattice, the selection of three colors and three generations, and the resulting two-input parameter economy. It also separates exact structural results, conditional continuum statements, calibration-sector outputs, and independent quantitative branches so that the status of each claim is explicit.

I believe this more focused presentation is better aligned with the aims of Foundations of Physics. The manuscript is directed at foundational questions in high-energy theory and gravitation: which elements of currently accepted effective physics can be derived from a smaller underlying axiom set, and which consequences of such a framework are sharp enough to be assessed on their merits.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has been prepared as a self-contained submission. I am the sole author and approve its submission to the journal.

Thank you for your consideration.
